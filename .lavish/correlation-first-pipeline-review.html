Implementation design Free-first Reviewable spec

Market sentiment research

Can news sentiment predict profitable trades?

A local, rerunnable pipeline to prove or falsify whether historical news sentiment predicts subsequent U.S. equity and ETF returns after timing constraints, baselines, costs, and an untouched holdout.

Vendor budget

$0

Massive, GDELT, Alpaca free tiers

Research window

2 years

1 year development + 1 year holdout

Initial universe

4 + 25

ETFs plus liquid large-cap equities

Primary interval

1 hour

15-minute secondary, daily baseline

Review first

Three questions determine whether this design is ready

  1. 1. Credibility Is a one-year untouched holdout enough for an initial go/no-go decision?
  2. 2. Leakage control Do the timestamp, next-bar, source, and observation rules prevent future information?
  3. 3. Decision value Are the stop, revise, and continue gates strong enough to guide the next investment?

01 / intent

Goal and scope boundary

V1 must prove

Usable out-of-sample signal

The design must go beyond correlation. A moderately realistic long/short/flat rule must remain interesting after next-bar execution, costs, controls, and a frozen holdout.

V1 does not claim

Production trading readiness

Two years can justify more research, but cannot prove robustness across many market regimes or support live brokerage execution.

Fixed decisions

Decision V1 choice Reason
Budget $0 vendors Prove acquisition and signal before paying for history.
Text Headline + summary Available legally and consistently from affordable feeds.
Sentiment Deterministic local scorer Explainable, versioned, and free to rerun.
Strategy Long / short / flat Tests positive and negative sentiment symmetrically.
Storage JSONL + Parquet + report files Immutable raw data with efficient reusable analysis.

Explicit non-goals

Live trading Interactive dashboard Tick data Futures and options Portfolio optimization Supervised ML Mandatory social data Production-grade proof

06 / credibility

Validation and execution realism

Year 1: development

Visible
  • Chronological exploration and walk-forward validation.
  • Select source sets, windows, horizons, and quantile thresholds.
  • Use time-aware uncertainty estimates for correlated intraday rows.
  • Freeze the primary matrix before viewing holdout results.

Year 2: final holdout

Untouched
  • Apply frozen thresholds and configuration exactly once.
  • Do not tune against holdout performance.
  • Label later holdout-driven variants exploratory.
  • Require new untouched data before calling those variants proof.

Backtest contract

  • Enter at the next configured tradable bar.
  • Never trade before the article publication timestamp.
  • Defer after-hours signals to the next regular session.
  • Long upper sentiment quantile; short lower quantile.
  • Remain flat in the middle of the distribution.
  • Report gross and net returns.
  • Apply configured spread/slippage costs.
  • Report long, short, and combined performance.

07 / safeguards

Data quality, failures, and tests

Pipeline safeguards

Normalize to UTC while retaining original timestamps.
Quarantine missing or ambiguous publication times.
Deduplicate syndicated and republished articles.
Respect holidays, early closes, after-hours, and daylight saving transitions.
Never silently forward-fill missing news or price bars.
Mark materially incomplete runs invalid, not merely negative.

Required test evidence

  • Temporal boundaries
    Windows, publication times, next-bar, after-hours
  • Market calendar
    Weekends, holidays, early close, daylight saving
  • Data correctness
    Deduplication, returns, short accounting, costs
  • Reproducibility
    Same snapshot, config, and seed yield same outputs
  • Traceability
    Raw article through signal, price bars, and trade
  • Controls
    Positive, negative, neutral, shuffled, end-to-end fixture
Failed experiments remain first-class artifacts with rate limits, retries, outages, dropped rows, and coverage gaps recorded.

09 / decision gates

What counts as success or failure?

Promising

Continue or expand

  • Held-out top-minus-bottom sentiment return spread is positive.
  • Held-out long/short rule is profitable after moderate costs.
  • Selected result remains profitable under doubled costs.
  • Development walk-forward periods show consistent direction.
  • No single symbol, source, week, or event dominates the result.
  • Tail-event prediction improves over non-sentiment baselines.
  • Coverage and timestamp checks pass.
Stop / revise

Do not scale yet

  • Coverage is too sparse or publication times are unreliable.
  • Sentiment does no better than shuffled or non-sentiment baselines.
  • The edge disappears under next-bar execution or simple costs.
  • Results depend on one fragile configuration or isolated event.

10 / delivery

Incremental stages

Every stage produces the same lineage, reports, controls, and explicit next decision.

0

Deterministic fixture pipeline

Recover known synthetic positive and negative relationships; prove reruns change only intended outputs.

1

Hand-curated real sample

Inspect timestamp alignment, after-hours handling, leakage controls, and traceability by hand.

2

Narrow free-source probe

Test Massive, GDELT, and Alpaca schemas, coverage, publisher diversity, limits, and missing-data behavior.

3

Free two-year hypothesis test

Freeze the primary experiment on year one, evaluate once on year two, and issue a go/no-go recommendation.

4

Conditional expansion

Buy more history or add predictive modeling only if Stage 3 survives costs, controls, and holdout validation.

11 / language

Core terminology

news_window
Historical articles included in a signal, such as trailing one hour or session-to-now.
signal_time
Time at which an aggregated signal becomes available for measurement or trading.
measurement_horizon
Future market period measured strictly after the signal time.
price_interval
Duration of each OHLCV price bar.
source_set
Configured mixture of finance, broad, and combined news used for an observation.
dataset_id
Immutable identifier for one source-data snapshot.
observation_set_id
Immutable identifier for signals derived from one dataset and aggregation configuration.
run_id
Identifier for one analysis or backtest configuration and its outputs.
